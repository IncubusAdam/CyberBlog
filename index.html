A Monster Made Real

Ransomeware, Cybersecurity

Ransomware always felt mystical to me. A far-off worry, never real. The idea that hackers could encrypt my data and demand payment from me to get it back was ridiculous. It happened to my brother’s employer, Nexon. Nexon cleans and distributes chemicals for New England, and during my brother’s time there they fell victim to ransomware. The systems that logged orders, both new and filled, were encrypted. They couldn’t work and were forced to menial upkeep or simply not working at all. The source was either unidentified or not disclosed, but hearing about it from my brother made ransomware real to me. This vague monster that lived in the back of my mind suddenly seemed clear. It only helped push me further toward cybersecurity and understanding the necessity of its practices.

Safe or Easy?

Accessability, Security

The balance of security and accessibility is a delicate one. Something that is 100% secure is not accessible and something that is 100% accessible is not secure. Somewhere in this middle ground is where companies must put their systems, their frontend services, their compounds, and their servers. The more I learn about what’s out there, the malicious actors and potential threats lurking, the more cautious I become. The more I learn toward security. Finding this happy medium, I feel will be a challenge, but one I am welcome to.
